Arduino Nano
 - Why this one?
    - The Arduino Nano was selected because it is inexpensive, compact, breadboard-friendly, and has sufficient processing power and I/O pins for reading biomedical sensors, displaying data on an OLED screen, and logging measurements to an SD card
 - Microcontroller: ATmega328P
 - Operating voltage: 5 V
 - Input voltage: 7–12 V through the VIN pin (when not powered by USB)
 - USB voltage: 5 V
 - Digital pins: 14 digital I/O pins (D0–D13), 6 support PWM (D3, D5, D6, D9, D10, D11)
 - Analog pins: 8 analog input pins (A0–A7), A6 and A7 are analog input only
 - Clock speed: 16 MHz
 - Flash memory: 32 KB
 - SRAM: 2 KB
 - EEPROM: 1 KB
 - Communication protocols: UART (Serial), I²C, SPI
